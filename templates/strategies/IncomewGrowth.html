Income with Growth Portfolio

Number of funds held: 4-8

Relative Volatility: 0.3 to 0.5

The Income with Growth Portfolio seeks to outperform inflation by investing in a low-volatility mix of stock funds and bond funds. Typically it will hold 4-8 funds, representing hundreds of unique stock positions and thousands of unique bond positions. Foreign exposure (both stocks and bonds) will usually be held to less than 30% of overall assets. Stock funds and bond funds from Fidelity’s retail lineup are normally used, but in an unusual situation we may opt to hold non-Fidelity funds if a particular category is not well-served under the Fidelity umbrella. The Income with Growth Portfolio normally blends stock funds and bond funds in an approximate 30/70 ratio, but we may vary that mix in order to stay on target from a volatility standpoint. The bond side of the portfolio will focus mainly on domestic investment-grade debt, but may include some exposure to highyield debt (both domestic and foreign), emerging country debt, real estate income securities, or inflationprotected bonds. The stock side of the portfolio will usually maintain a balanced mix of domestic large-cap stocks, but may include some exposure to smaller stocks, foreign stocks, convertibles, real estate equity securities, or commodities. The portfolio does not usually invest in municipal bond funds. To manage volatility on the bond side, we consider the market’s sensitivity to both credit risk and interest-rate risk, seeking to hold a combination of funds that will offset the portfolio’s equity risk to the greatest degree possible, while delivering income that has the potential to keep the bond side growing at the rate of inflation or higher. To manage volatility on the stock side, we consider valuation levels across the stylebox in combination with volatility levels, seeking to hold a mix of funds that will meet the portfolio’s overall volatility target while providing a degree of protection against rising interest rates and giving the portfolio the potential to finish significantly ahead of inflation over the long run. The Income with Growth Portfolio normally holds actively managed funds, but may invest in index funds during periods of high uncertainty (or if we believe market conditions make it difficult for active managers to outperform their indexes). During bearish market conditions, the portfolio may reduce its equity exposure or place a significant portion of its bond holdings in short-term funds, but normally we will resist any market timing moves. Because this portfolio holds both stocks and bonds and maintains a relatively low risk blend of those asset classes, the risk of loss is not usually as great as other portfolios that carry a higher stock weighting or lack inflation protection.
